Literature Review

Existing research shows that Gen Z interacts with brands very differently compared to previous generations. Studies highlight that Gen Z prefers fast, visual, and authentic content, especially on platforms like TikTok. Unlike traditional advertising, Gen Z is more likely to engage with content that feels relatable and natural rather than overly produced or promotional. This shift has changed how brands approach marketing and communication.

Research on TikTok emphasizes the role of the platform’s algorithm in rapidly spreading trends. Short-form videos and visually engaging content make it easy for trends to gain popularity quickly. This creates an environment where aesthetics, visuals, and storytelling become key tools for capturing attention. As a result, TikTok has become a powerful platform for influencing not only what users see, but also how they perceive brands and products.

Studies on aesthetics and consumer behavior suggest that visual appeal plays a major role in shaping perception and purchase intention. Aesthetics such as color, style, and overall visual identity can create emotional connections with consumers. For Gen Z, aesthetics are closely tied to identity and self-expression, meaning that the way a brand looks can influence whether it feels relevant or appealing.

However, while existing research explores social media marketing, Gen Z behavior, and visual design separately, there is less focus on how specific TikTok-driven aesthetics like clean girl, coquette, and Y2K directly influence brand perception. This project aims to explore that gap by connecting aesthetic trends to real brand examples and consumer behavior.
