Y2K Aesthetic

What it is: The Y2K aesthetic brings back early 2000s fashion through bold colors, metallic elements, low-rise styles, and playful graphics. On TikTok, this trend has been revived through nostalgic and visually engaging content.

Key features: Bright colors, shiny textures, baby tees, mini bags, chunky accessories, and futuristic or digital-inspired visuals.

Why it appeals to Gen Z: Many Gen Z consumers were too young to experience the early 2000s, so the trend feels both nostalgic and new. TikTok makes it easy to recreate and share these looks, which increases its popularity.

Brand connection: Brands have adapted this aesthetic into their marketing to stay relevant. For example, Canva incorporates Y2K-inspired visuals in its TikTok content, using bold colors and retro design elements to connect with Gen Z audiences.

Additional examples: This aesthetic is often seen in outfits like baby tees paired with low-rise jeans and mini shoulder bags. Accessories such as chunky rings, tinted sunglasses, and metallic handbags are also common. In digital content, creators use sparkles, chrome text, and bright color gradients to recreate the early internet style.

On TikTok, many users recreate early 2000s outfits, showing how the Y2K aesthetic has become popular again among Gen Z.

Coquette Aesthetic

What it is: The coquette aesthetic focuses on femininity, softness, and romantic visuals. It often includes bows, lace, pastel tones, and delicate makeup.

Key features: Light pink and white color palettes, ribbons, vintage-inspired clothing, soft hairstyles, and subtle, natural-looking makeup.

Why it appeals to Gen Z: This trend allows users to express a softer and more emotional identity. On TikTok, it is often shown through outfit styling videos and aesthetic edits that create a dreamy and nostalgic feeling.

Brand connection: Many fashion and beauty brands use coquette elements to appeal to audiences who value storytelling and aesthetics. This helps brands feel more personal and visually engaging rather than purely commercial.

Additional examples: This aesthetic appears in outfits with lace tops, bows, and soft pink or white clothing. In beauty content, it includes blush-heavy makeup, glossy lips, and natural hairstyles. TikTok creators often use soft lighting, romantic music, and slow edits to create a dreamy and emotional atmosphere.

Retail stores have also adapted the coquette aesthetic by incorporating soft colors, bows, and feminine styles into their collections, showing how trends influence what is available to consumers.

Clean Girl Aesthetic

What it is: The clean girl aesthetic focuses on a polished and minimal look that emphasizes natural beauty, simple outfits, and a put-together appearance. On TikTok, it is often presented through routines that make the look feel effortless.

Key features: Slicked-back hair, glowing skin, neutral clothing, gold jewelry, and light makeup that enhances natural features.

Why it appeals to Gen Z: This aesthetic promotes simplicity and self-care, making it feel both aspirational and achievable. TikTok has helped popularize it through “get ready with me” videos and skincare routines.

Brand connection: The clean girl aesthetic aligns closely with beauty and skincare brands, especially those focused on hydration, glow, and minimal makeup. It helps brands appear modern, effortless, and relatable.

Additional examples: This aesthetic is commonly seen in simple outfits like white tops, neutral tones, and gold accessories. In beauty content, it includes dewy skin, brushed brows, clear lip gloss, and sleek hairstyles.

On TikTok, this aesthetic is often shared through makeup routines and skincare-focused content that highlight glowing skin and a natural look.

In addition to beauty routines, the clean girl aesthetic is also reflected in simple, neutral outfits that emphasize a polished and effortless look.
