Research Methodology

This project uses a qualitative approach to explore how TikTok-driven aesthetics influence Gen Z’s perception of brands and purchasing behavior. The main method used is content analysis, focusing on visual trends, brand representation, and user engagement across TikTok and digital platforms.

The analysis began by identifying three major TikTok aesthetics: clean girl, coquette, and Y2K. These trends were selected because of their popularity and strong visual identity. TikTok content, including screenshots and examples, was analyzed to understand how these aesthetics are presented through colors, styling, and overall visual tone.

In addition to trend analysis, this project examines how brands apply these aesthetics in their marketing. Brands such as Rhode, Bershka, and Canva were selected as examples because they reflect different approaches to aesthetic-driven branding. Their websites and social media content were analyzed to identify how they incorporate visual trends to appeal to Gen Z audiences.

The project also incorporates academic and industry sources to support the analysis. These sources provide background on Gen Z behavior, social media marketing, and consumer psychology. The website itself was built using HTML and CSS to present both written and visual content in a structured and engaging way.

AI tools, including ChatGPT, were used to assist with structuring content, refining writing, and improving the layout of the website. While AI supported the process, all final decisions, analysis, and interpretations were made independently. This combination of tools and methods allowed for a clear and accessible exploration of the research question.
