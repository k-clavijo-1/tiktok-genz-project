Brands and Trend Influence

This section looks at how brands use TikTok-driven aesthetics in their marketing and visual identity. While these trends begin as styles and forms of self-expression, they also shape how brands present themselves to Gen Z audiences. When a brand reflects an aesthetic that feels familiar or popular on TikTok, it can seem more current, relatable, and appealing.

These examples show that aesthetics like Y2K, coquette, and clean girl do not just influence what Gen Z wears or posts online. They also influence how brands design content, promote products, and connect with consumers in ways that can affect brand perception and purchase behavior.

Canva and the Y2K Aesthetic

Canva is a design platform that is widely used by students, creators, and brands to make digital content. Because its audience includes many young users, Canva often adapts popular visual trends into its own content. One example of this is its use of Y2K-inspired design elements on TikTok.

In this example, Canva uses bold typography, sparkles, and retro-inspired graphics to reflect the Y2K aesthetic. These design choices make the post feel playful, nostalgic, and visually similar to the trend-based content that Gen Z already engages with on TikTok. Rather than feeling overly formal or corporate, the brand appears current and involved in internet culture.

This matters because Gen Z is especially responsive to content that feels relevant and native to the platform. When Canva uses a trend like Y2K in its marketing, it can strengthen brand perception by making the company feel more creative, relatable, and in touch with what users like. In turn, that can encourage engagement with the brand and increase interest in using its design tools for similar content.

Bershka and the Coquette Aesthetic

Bershka campaign featuring soft colors and feminine styling that reflect the coquette aesthetic.

Bershka is a fast-fashion brand that targets a younger audience, making it highly responsive to trends that gain popularity on platforms like TikTok. One of the aesthetics the brand has leaned into is the coquette aesthetic, which emphasizes femininity, softness, and romantic visuals.

In this campaign, Bershka uses light colors, delicate fabrics, and soft styling to reflect the coquette aesthetic. The overall look feels minimal but still very intentional, creating a romantic and slightly nostalgic vibe that aligns with the content many Gen Z users engage with online.

For a period of time, Bershka even had a coquette section on its website, grouping together pieces that matched this aesthetic. Although that section is no longer available, it shows how the brand directly translated a TikTok trend into the way it organized and promoted its products.

This approach can influence how Gen Z perceives the brand. By reflecting a popular aesthetic, Bershka feels more aligned with current trends and more relevant to its audience. This can make consumers more likely to browse the brand’s products and purchase items that help them recreate the look they see on TikTok.

Rhode and the Clean Girl Aesthetic

Rhode is a skincare brand that closely reflects the clean girl aesthetic through its focus on glowing skin, minimal makeup, and simple routines. Because the brand is strongly connected to Hailey Bieber, who is often associated with this aesthetic, Rhode has become one of the clearest examples of how a trend can shape a brand’s identity.

In this image, the brand is connected to the polished and effortless look that defines the clean girl aesthetic. The glowing skin, minimal makeup, simple headband, and neutral styling all reflect the kind of beauty content that performs well on TikTok. The image does not just sell a product. It sells the look and lifestyle that many Gen Z consumers want to recreate.

This is important because clean girl content often encourages viewers to follow routines, use specific products, and connect beauty with self-care and simplicity. By aligning itself so closely with this aesthetic, Rhode can make the brand feel aspirational but still attainable. That can strengthen brand perception and increase purchase intention, because consumers are not only buying skincare but also buying into the image associated with it.
